Colors in ASL

Learn how to sign different colors in American Sign Language. Colors are fundamental vocabulary for describing objects, clothing, nature, and expressing creativity.

Red
Index finger on lips, then move down
üí° Remember: Like lipstick motion
Usage: Red car, red apple, red shirt
Blue
B handshape, shake side to side
üí° Remember: B for Blue
Usage: Blue sky, blue jeans, blue eyes
Yellow
Y handshape, shake side to side
üí° Remember: Y for Yellow
Usage: Yellow sun, yellow banana, yellow flower
Green
G handshape, shake side to side
üí° Remember: G for Green
Usage: Green grass, green tree, green vegetables
Orange
C handshape at mouth, squeeze to S
üí° Remember: Squeezing an orange
Usage: Orange fruit, orange sunset, orange pumpkin
Purple
P handshape, shake side to side
üí° Remember: P for Purple
Usage: Purple grapes, purple flowers, purple dress
Pink
P handshape, brush down chin twice
üí° Remember: P brushing like blush
Usage: Pink roses, pink shirt, pink clouds
Black
Index finger across forehead
üí° Remember: Drawing a dark line
Usage: Black car, black hair, black shoes
White
Open hand on chest, pull out to fingertips
üí° Remember: Pulling white from heart
Usage: White clouds, white snow, white shirt
Brown
B handshape, slide down side of face
üí° Remember: B sliding down cheek
Usage: Brown bear, brown eyes, brown hair
Gray
Open hands, fingers pass through each other
üí° Remember: Mixing black and white
Usage: Gray clouds, gray cat, gray building

üé® Color Sign Patterns

Letter-Based Colors

Many colors use their first letter + shaking motion:

  • Blue: B + shake
  • Yellow: Y + shake
  • Green: G + shake
  • Purple: P + shake

Visual Association Colors

These colors relate to visual or physical associations:

  • Red: Like applying lipstick
  • Orange: Like squeezing an orange
  • Pink: Like applying blush
  • Black: Drawing across forehead
