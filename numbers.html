Numbers 0-10 in ASL

Master the fundamental numbers in American Sign Language. These are essential for dates, times, ages, quantities, and many daily conversations.

0
Zero
Make an "O" shape with thumb and fingers
💡 Tip: Similar to "OK" hand gesture
1
One
Index finger up, others closed
💡 Tip: Point to the sky
2
Two
Index and middle finger up, peace sign
💡 Tip: Victory or peace gesture
3
Three
Thumb, index, and middle finger up
💡 Tip: Add thumb to number 2
4
Four
Four fingers up, thumb tucked
💡 Tip: All fingers except thumb
5
Five
All five fingers spread open
💡 Tip: Open palm, fingers apart
6
Six
Thumb touches pinky, others up
💡 Tip: Thumb and pinky make a circle
7
Seven
Thumb touches ring finger
💡 Tip: Move thumb to ring finger
8
Eight
Thumb touches middle finger
💡 Tip: Thumb to middle finger
9
Nine
Thumb touches index finger
💡 Tip: Thumb and index make small circle
10
Ten
Thumb up, shake hand side to side
💡 Tip: Thumbs up with wiggle motion

🎯 Practice Exercises

Counting Practice

  • Sequential Counting: Count from 0 to 10, then backwards
  • Random Numbers: Practice signing random numbers someone calls out
  • Age Practice: Sign your age and family members' ages
  • Time Practice: Sign the hour when telling time

Real-World Applications

  • Phone Numbers: Practice signing individual digits
  • Addresses: Sign house or apartment numbers
  • Quantities: "I want 3 apples" or "2 cups of coffee"
  • Money: Sign dollar amounts under $10

🔢 Number Patterns to Remember

0-5: Basic Patterns

Numbers 0-5 use simple finger counting that feels natural and intuitive.

6-9: Thumb Touch

Numbers 6-9 all involve the thumb touching different fingers, starting from pinky to index.

10: Special Motion

Number 10 is unique with a thumbs-up and wiggling motion to distinguish it from number 1.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Number 9 vs 0: Make sure the circle is smaller for 9, larger for 0
  • Number 6-9 clarity: Ensure thumb clearly touches the correct finger
  • Number 10 motion: Don't forget the side-to-side shake
  • Palm direction: Keep palm facing outward toward the person you're signing to
