Family & People in ASL

Learn how to sign family members and describe people in American Sign Language. These signs are essential for talking about relationships and social connections.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Immediate Family

Mother / Mom
Open hand touches chin, then moves down
💡 Remember: Female sign + parent motion
Father / Dad
Open hand touches forehead, then moves down
💡 Remember: Male sign + parent motion
Sister
Thumb on chin, then index fingers together
💡 Female + same (siblings)
Brother
Thumb on forehead, then index fingers together
💡 Male + same (siblings)
Son
Male sign, then baby cradling motion
💡 Male + baby
Daughter
Female sign, then baby cradling motion
💡 Female + baby

👵👴 Extended Family

Grandmother
Female sign, then two forward bounces
💡 Mother + generation back
Grandfather
Male sign, then two forward bounces
💡 Father + generation back
Aunt
A handshape near cheek, shake
💡 A for Aunt, female side
Uncle
U handshape near temple, shake
💡 U for Uncle, male side
Cousin
C handshape, shake at temple or cheek
💡 C for Cousin, choose side for gender

💑 Relationships

Husband
Male sign, then hands clasped together
💡 Male + marriage bond
Wife
Female sign, then hands clasped together
💡 Female + marriage bond
Boyfriend
B handshape on forehead, then friend sign
💡 Boy + friend relationship
Girlfriend
G handshape on chin, then friend sign
💡 Girl + friend relationship
Friend
Index fingers hook together, then switch
💡 Mutual connection and bond

👤 General People

Person
P handshapes move down parallel
💡 P for Person, outline of body
Man
Thumb on forehead, then down to chest
💡 Male sign + person
Woman
Thumb on chin, then down to chest
💡 Female sign + person
Boy
Fingertips snap at forehead (like cap)
💡 Like tipping a baseball cap
Girl
Thumb brushes along jawline
💡 Like tying bonnet strings
Baby
Cradling motion, rock side to side
💡 Universal rocking a baby motion

🔍 Family Sign Patterns

Gender Markers

  • Male: Forehead area (father, brother, man)
  • Female: Chin/jaw area (mother, sister, woman)

Generation Indicators

  • Same generation: Side-to-side or together motions
  • Older generation: Forward bouncing motions
  • Younger generation: Downward or cradling motions

💬 Family Conversations

🏠 Talking About Family

Q: "How many people in your family?"

A: "My family has 4 people: mother, father, sister, and me."

👶 Describing Relationships

Statement: "This is my baby sister. She is 2 years old."

💑 Introducing Partners

Introduction: "I want you to meet my girlfriend/boyfriend."

🎯 Practice Activities

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Family Tree

Draw your family tree and practice signing each relationship as you point to them.

📸 Photo Description

Look at family photos and describe who each person is using ASL signs.

🎭 Role Play

Practice introducing family members in different scenarios (formal/informal).
